Coursework

PSY 1100 — General Psychology

Core

Foundations of psychology including biological bases of behavior, sensation and perception, states of consciousness, learning, memory, cognition, motivation and emotion, developmental psychology, personality, psychological disorders, and social behavior.

PSY 2200 — Statistics for the Behavioral Sciences

Statistics

Introduction to statistical methods used in psychological research. Topics include descriptive statistics, probability, hypothesis testing, t-tests, ANOVA, correlation, and regression. Emphasis on interpretation and critical evaluation of data.

PSY 3090 — Personality Psychology

Personality

Survey of major theories of personality including psychoanalytic, humanistic, trait, behavioral, and cognitive-social approaches. Topics include personality assessment, research methods, and the role of culture and biology in shaping personality.

PSY 309 — Statistics Lab Assignments

Lab

Hands-on application of statistical concepts using real datasets. Assignments included computing descriptive statistics, running t-tests and ANOVAs, interpreting output, and writing APA-style results sections.

Reflections

What I Learned

This course challenged me to think more rigorously and empathetically. Statistics taught me to question assumptions and demand evidence. Personality psychology reminded me that behind every data point is a human being with a complex inner world. Together, these disciplines have made me a more thoughtful communicator and a more curious person.

Personal Growth

I came into this course thinking I already understood people pretty well. I left realizing how much I had to learn. The theories we studied — from the Big Five to Duckworth’s grit — gave me frameworks I now use daily. I see personality differences more clearly. I evaluate arguments more carefully. I listen more intentionally.

Looking Forward

The lessons from this course will carry into my career in communications and beyond. Understanding behavior, motivation, and individual differences is essential whether I’m crafting a message, building a team, or navigating conflict. This portfolio is not just a record of work completed — it is a snapshot of who I am becoming.
